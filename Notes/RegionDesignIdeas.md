## **1. Hub Kingdom (tentative name)**

**Inspiration:** European (broadly medieval, but not locked to one country)

**Key Features:**
* Rolling farmland, rivers, forests, and a central castle city
* Smaller villages with local specialties (wine, wool, milling, etc.)

**Population:**
* Predominantly lighter-skinned populations, but not homogeneous
* Noticeable presence of travelers, diplomats, and merchants from other regions

**Quests / Flavor:**
* Local disputes (land, inheritance, guild conflicts)
* Seasonal festivals (harvest, solstice, etc.)
* A foreign artisan introducing a new craft causes tension with local guilds

**Cultural Depth:**
* Strong guild systems and class structures
* Religion or spiritual practices tied to light, harvest, or protection
* Food, clothing, and architecture vary subtly between towns (don’t make it one-note "generic medieval")

## **2. Sunscorched Plateau**

**Inspiration:** African savannah and desert cultures (avoid collapsing into a single influence or pushing negative stereotypes about Africa)

**Key Features:**
* Expansive savannahs, baobab-like trees, rocky plateaus, oasis settlements
* Trade routes carved through ecologically rich but punishing environments

**Population:**
* Primarily Black communities with varied identities (nomadic, settled, scholarly, pastoral)
* Caravan networks connect distant settlements

**Quests / Flavor:**
* Traveling with a caravan to learn how to survive in the rich but punishing landscape
* Participating in a festival that celebrates the return of specific wind patterns that enable travel, pollination, and migration
* A storyteller whose tales are “coming true” in unexpected—and sometimes dangerous—ways

**Cultural Depth:**
* Oral traditions are central—history is preserved through storytellers, not books
* Music and rhythm are embedded in daily life (work songs, ceremonial drumming)
* Clothing is practical but expressive—color, pattern, and jewelry signal identity and status
* Magic may be tied to ancestry, memory, or the land itself

## **3. Tall Palm Tropics**

**Inspiration:** Polynesian + Indigenous South American (handled carefully—focus on how environment shapes culture)

**Key Features:**
* Archipelagos, coastal jungles, river systems, and elevated settlements
* Canoe travel, rope bridges, and tree-integrated architecture

**Population:**
* Seafaring and river-based communities
* Strong navigational knowledge and environmental awareness

**Quests / Flavor:**
* Navigating between islands using stars or currents
* Protecting sacred groves or reef ecosystems
* A coming-of-age journey tied to exploration or survival

**Cultural Depth:**
* Deep relationship with ocean and forest—both are alive in belief systems
* Tattoos, carvings, and body art carry personal and communal meaning
* Music and storytelling are tied to movement (dance, paddling rhythms, chants)
* Magic may be tied to wind, water, and navigation

## **4. Frostpeak Highlands**

**Inspiration:** Inuit and Norse/Viking cultures (distinct, coexisting—not blended into one)

**Key Features:**
* Snowfields, fjords, ice caves, northern lights
* Two distinct settlement styles: coastal (Inuit-inspired) and inland/fortified (Norse-inspired)

**Population:**
* Two culturally distinct groups with different traditions, technologies, and worldviews
* Trade and mutual reliance create cooperation without erasing differences

**Quests / Flavor:**
* Mediating misunderstandings between the two groups
* Survival-focused quests (hunting, shelter, weather prediction)
* Investigating a disturbance affecting migratory animals or sea routes

**Cultural Depth:**
* Inuit-inspired communities: deep ecological knowledge, efficient survival techniques
* Norse-inspired communities: seafaring, craftsmanship, oral sagas
* Shared respect forged through necessity—not forced harmony
* Magic may differ between groups (e.g., spirit-guided vs. rune-based)

## **5. Seas and Sands**

**Inspiration:** Arabic and South Asian cultures

**Key Features:**
* Coastal trade cities, deserts, spice markets, and inland caravan routes
* Dense urban centers contrasted with vast open deserts

**Population:**
* Highly diverse due to trade—this is one of your most cosmopolitan regions
* Merchants, scholars, sailors, and mystics

**Quests / Flavor:**
* Trade negotiations, rare goods acquisition
* Political intrigue within merchant houses
* A mystical phenomenon tied to sandstorms or the sea

**Cultural Depth:**
* Strong emphasis on hospitality, trade ethics, and reputation
* Rich culinary culture (spices, layered dishes, tea traditions)
* Architecture: domes, courtyards, shaded walkways
* Magic may be scholarly (texts, astronomy) or mystical (djinn-like entities, but tread carefully and respectfully)

---

## **6. Jade Peaks**

**Inspiration:** East Asian cultures (again, not just one country analogue)

**Key Features:**
* Mountain ranges, terraced fields, bamboo forests, misty valleys
* Monasteries, scholarly centers, and quiet villages

**Population:**
* Farmers, monks, artisans, scholars
* Strong regional identity within the area itself

**Quests / Flavor:**
* Training under a master or learning a discipline
* Resolving spiritual imbalance in a village
* Political tension between tradition and modernization

**Cultural Depth:**
* Philosophical systems influence daily life (balance, harmony, discipline)
* Art forms (calligraphy, painting, martial arts) are culturally significant
* Structured social roles, but room for personal growth narratives
* Magic may be tied to balance, energy flow, or spiritual cultivation
