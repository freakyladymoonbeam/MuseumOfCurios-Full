# Quest Flow: Eternal Vows

---

## PHASE 0: Setup (Passive Trigger)

### Player Action
- Player obtains **Tarnished Ring (Common)**
- Player later uses a polishing item → upgrades to **Restored Ring (Rare)**
- Player displays the polished ring in the museum

### Result
- After a few in-game days, **Julia appears** near the display

---

## PHASE 1: Recognition & Hook

### Scene: Museum Floor

**Julia’s Role:**
- Immediately recognizes the ring as her grandparents’ work
- Explains:
  - It is one half of a wedding pair
  - Made for **Lilia and Balthazar**
- Mentions:
  - Her grandparents kept the molds as their masterpiece

**Quest Hook:**
> “There should be another ring. If we can find it… we can reunite them.”

**Player Goal Introduced:**
- Locate the second ring

**Tone:**
- Curious, reverent, optimistic

---

## PHASE 2: Investigation Begins

### Gameplay Loop
- Player and Julia investigate locations tied to:
  - The jewellers (her grandparents)
  - The wedding
  - The noble estate

### Spectral Encounters (Fragmented)

#### Spectre 1: The Workshop
- Warm, proud moment of the rings being crafted
- Emphasis on:
  - Care, precision, shared effort

#### Spectre 2: The Wedding
- Joyful but slightly tense atmosphere
- Subtle hints:
  - Political unease
  - Fragility beneath celebration

**Julia’s State:**
- Enthusiastic, emotionally invested
- Speaks of the rings as “perfect,” “meant to endure”

---

## PHASE 3: Collapse of the Story

### Gameplay Progression
- Investigation leads to records/fragments about war

#### Spectre 3: The Estate’s Fall
- Chaos, urgency, loss
- Balthazar’s absence implied or confirmed

#### Spectre 4: The Escape
- Lilia fleeing
- Clutching her ring
- Fear, grief, determination

**Key Revelation:**
- Lilia was buried with her ring

---

## PHASE 4: Conflict Emerges

### Scene: After the Revelation

**Julia’s Reaction:**
- Hesitation for the first time
- Attempts to reconcile:
  - “If we reunite them… isn’t that honoring them?”

**Core Dilemma Introduced:**
> The ring is not lost—it was laid to rest.

**Player Objective Updates:**
- Travel to Lilia’s burial site

**Tone Shift:**
- From discovery → moral weight

---

## PHASE 5: The Grave

### Scene: Lilia’s Resting Place

**Atmosphere:**
- Quiet, still, reverent
- Strong pause in pacing

---

### Spectral Encounter: Lilia

**Form:**
- Incomplete, memory-like presence
- No explicit dialogue

---

### Key Moment

- The restored ring (Balthazar’s) reacts:
  - Subtle warmth or resonance

- Lilia’s spirit appears in response:
  - Not blocking
  - Not encouraging

---

### Memory Fragment (Optional but Strong)
- Lilia choosing to keep the ring with her
- A deliberate, personal act

---

### Player Action
- Player retrieves (or is allowed to retrieve) the ring

**Important Framing:**
- No explicit “permission”
- The act feels:
  - Considered
  - Quiet
  - Slightly heavy

---

### Julia’s Response
- Uncertain, reflective:
  - “I think… she’s at peace.”
  - “Or maybe… she’s already let go.”

---

## PHASE 6: Reunion

### Scene: Museum or Workshop

- Both rings are placed together

**Effect:**
- A soft, uneven resonance
- Not triumphant—gentle, enduring

---

### Julia’s Resolution

She shifts from:
> “They were meant to be together.”

To:
> “They were never truly lost… just changed.”

**Final Understanding:**
- Preservation is not restoration
- Meaning comes from what remains

---

## PHASE 7: Reward

### Player Receives:
- **Epic Curio: Eternal Vows**

---

### Curio Presentation

- Rings displayed together, but distinct
- Visible differences:
  - Wear, damage, history

**Subtle Magic:**
- Gentle resonance when near each other

---

## PHASE 8: Epilogue (Optional but Recommended)

### Julia Follow-Up

- More at ease, less rigid about “perfection”
- May speak about:
  - Creating something new
  - Letting objects carry their stories forward

---

## THEMATIC THROUGHLINE

- Objects do not remain perfect—they accumulate meaning  
- Love does not need a perfect ending to endure  
- Preservation is not about restoring the past, but **honoring its truth**  

---

## DESIGN NOTES

- Avoid explicit moral resolution from Lilia’s spirit  
- Let ambiguity persist in Julia’s interpretation  
- Use silence and pacing at the grave to carry emotional weight  
- Ensure the second ring visually reflects its history (not pristine)
