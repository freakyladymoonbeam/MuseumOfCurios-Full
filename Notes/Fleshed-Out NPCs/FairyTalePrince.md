# Character Sheet: Fairy-Tale Inspired Swordsman

---

## Basic Info
- **Name:** [Something appropriate for a fairy-tale prince]
- **Age:** Late 20s
- **Gender/Identity:** Transmasc, fully comfortable
- **Occupation/Skill:** Trained swordsman, well-regarded in the city
- **Personality:** Chivalrous, courteous, princely, but self-aware; consciously models life after a beloved fairy tale prince

---

## Appearance / Visual Notes
- Well-groomed, princely attire without being overly ostentatious
- Carries a sword
- Calm, composed posture, but expressive face
- Optional detail: subtle emblem or motif referencing his favorite fairy tale

---

## Core Trait
- **Self-Curated Identity:** He lives deliberately according to the ideals of a storybook prince, balancing morality, nobility, and kindness

---

## Quest Theme
- **Core Question:** What happens when someone models their life after a fairy tale and reality doesn’t follow the script?
- **Quest Arcs:**
  1. **Broken Fairy Tale:** Investigate the original tale’s true origins; reconcile ideals with reality
  2. **Unfinished Tale:** Discover the tale had no ending; “write” your own ending through actions
  3. **False Prince Rival:** Confront someone claiming the prince’s mantle; define nobility by deeds, not recognition

---

## Romance Arc
- Player sees both the performance and the person underneath
- Key moments:
  - Drops “storybook voice” in private
  - Admits uncertainty about always doing the “right thing”
  - Learns that self-worth and love aren’t dependent on perfection

---

## Epic Curio Reward
| Curio | Description | Significance |
|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **The Unwritten Ending** | Blank storybook that fills itself based on player choices | Represents self-authorship, collaborative storytelling, identity creation |
| **The Prince’s Oathblade** | Ceremonial sword with inscriptions that change based on noble actions | Reinforces nobility through deeds, ties to swordsmanship |
| **The Crown That Was Never Worn** | Fragile crown reacting to acts of selflessness | Symbolic of chosen identity vs inherited status |
| **Mirror of True Form** | Reflects inner alignment, not physical form | Metaphor for internal vs external identity |
| **The First Page** | Preserved page from oldest version of the tale | Grounds quest in myth/history, lightly magical |

> Recommended Curio: **The Unwritten Ending** – aligns best with his identity and quest themes  

---

## Design Notes / Tips
- Keep tension alive: he struggles when “doing the right thing” isn’t clear  
- Moments of vulnerability are crucial for making him engaging and romanceable  
- Subtle magical/fairy tale elements reinforce the quest’s theme without overshadowing character depth  

---

## Dialogue Hooks
- Opening line: “I’ve always admired princes in stories… I wonder what it truly means to be one.”  
- Vulnerable moment: “Sometimes I fear I’m just acting the part, not living it.”  
- Quest completion: “Perhaps the ending we choose is the one worth telling.”
